CULTURE: Indian Folklore
SOURCE: Encyclopedia of Myths

[FESTIVALS]
Diwali: The Festival of Lights. Celebrates the return of Lord Rama to Ayodhya after defeating the demon king Ravana. People light diyas (oil lamps) to guide him home. It symbolizes the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.
Holi: The Festival of Colors. Celebrates the victory of Narasimha over Hiranyakashipu and the burning of Holika. It involves playing with colored powders (gulal).
Navratri: A nine-night festival dedicated to the Goddess Durga. Each day represents a different avatar of the goddess.

[SYMBOLS]
Lotus (Kamal): Symbolizes purity, divine beauty, and detachment. Even though it grows in mud, it remains unstained.
Peacock (Mayura): The vehicle (vahana) of Lord Kartikeya. Represents beauty and grace.
Banyan Tree (Vat Vriksha): Represents eternal life and longevity due to its ever-expanding roots.

[VALUES]
Dharma: Duty, righteousness, and cosmic order.
Karma: The law of cause and effect. Every action has a reaction.
Atithi Devo Bhava: The guest is equivalent to God. Hospitality is a sacred duty.

[MYTHICAL CREATURES]
Garuda: A divine eagle-like sun bird and the king of birds. He is the mount of Lord Vishnu.
Nagas: Divine serpent beings that live in the netherworld (Patala). They guard treasures.
Yakshas: Nature spirits, caretakers of natural treasures hidden in the earth and tree roots.
